Note that the "groupplot" feature can be used to stack graphs:

\hspace{1in}
\begin{tikzpicture}
\begin{groupplot}[
 	group style={
      		group size=1 by 2,
		xlabels at=edge bottom,
		xticklabels at=edge bottom,
		vertical sep=0.2in
		},
	height=2in, width=2in,
]
\nextgroupplot[
	lab_noticks_2quads,
	xlabel={time (s)},
	ymin=0,
	ylabel={position (m)},
]
\nextgroupplot[
	lab_noticks_2quads,
	plus_minus_zero_labels,
	xlabel={time (s)},
	ylabel={velocity (m/s)},
]
\end{groupplot}
\end{tikzpicture}

Finally, you can drop alignment marks between graphs, as the next two examples show:

\begin{tikzpicture}

    group style={
        group size=1 by 2,

        xticklabels at=edge bottom,

    },
    width=4.5in,
    height=2in,
    xlabel=time (s),
    xmin=0, xmax=9,
]

\nextgroupplot[ymax=15, ylabel={position (m)}]
\addplot coordinates{(0,1) (1,1)};
